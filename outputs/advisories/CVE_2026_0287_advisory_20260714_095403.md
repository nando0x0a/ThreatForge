# Security Advisory: Palo Alto Networks Firewall Denial of Service Vulnerabilities

**Advisory Status:** Proposed Draft for Analyst Review
**Reference:** CVE-2026-0287
**Priority Tier:** HIGH PRIORITY
**Issued:** 3 days after vulnerability disclosure

---

## Executive Summary

Palo Alto Networks firewall software (PAN-OS) has been found to contain multiple security weaknesses that allow an external attacker — with no credentials or special access — to crash or disable the firewall's traffic-processing capabilities by sending it specially crafted network data. This affects any organisation running exposed Palo Alto firewall interfaces on their network perimeter. Severity ratings from independent sources differ: the U.S. National Vulnerability Database rates this as **High severity (7.5/10)** [1], while the vendor's own published record rates it **Medium severity (6.6/10)** [2][3] — analysts should review this discrepancy when calibrating response urgency (see note below). Regardless of which rating applies, the vulnerability is remotely exploitable with no authentication required, and immediate remediation action is recommended.

> ⚠️ **Severity Rating Discrepancy:** Two authoritative sources disagree on the severity of this vulnerability. The U.S. National Vulnerability Database (NVD) assigns a CVSS score of **7.5 (HIGH)** [1], while the CVE.org CNA-published record using the newer CVSS v4.0 scoring standard assigns **6.6 (MEDIUM)** [3]. The vendor advisory rates it at **6.6 (MEDIUM)** [2]. Management and security teams should be aware of this disagreement. This advisory applies a conservative posture and treats the higher rating as the planning baseline until an analyst confirms otherwise.

---

## Business Impact

If this vulnerability is left unaddressed, the following business risks apply:

- **Service Disruption:** An attacker can remotely disable the firewall's ability to process network traffic. Depending on your network architecture, this could result in a complete outage of internet connectivity, VPN access for remote workers, or access to critical business applications — with no warning and no authentication required from the attacker.

- **Security Posture Degradation:** A disabled or crashed firewall ceases to enforce security policies. During any period where the firewall is non-functional, the organisation's internal network may be exposed to further attacks that would normally be blocked.

- **Operational Continuity Risk:** Repeated or sustained attacks could cause prolonged downtime. Recovery from firewall crashes requires IT intervention, and repeated exploitation could result in extended periods of reduced network protection or business interruption.

- **Regulatory and Compliance Exposure:** Organisations subject to data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR, PCI-DSS, ISO 27001) have obligations to maintain effective security controls. A known, unpatched vulnerability in a critical security control could constitute a compliance gap, with potential audit or reporting consequences should an incident occur.

- **Reputational Risk:** Outages affecting customers, partners, or public-facing services resulting from this vulnerability could damage trust and organisational reputation.

## Affected Systems

The following Palo Alto Networks products are affected [1][2]:

- **Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS** — the operating system that runs on Palo Alto Networks next-generation firewalls
- Specifically, systems with **dataplane interfaces exposed to network traffic** are the entry point for exploitation

> ℹ️ *Specific affected PAN-OS version ranges were not confirmed in the available advisory detail at the time of this advisory's preparation. IT teams should consult the Palo Alto Networks security advisory directly to identify exact version numbers and confirm whether their deployed versions are in scope* [2].

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate the following actions immediately**, given the HIGH PRIORITY classification and the fact that this vulnerability is only 3 days old with active risk:

1. **Authorise Emergency Patching:** Direct the IT/Security team to prioritise patching of all PAN-OS firewalls in line with the vendor's guidance [2]. This should be treated as an out-of-cycle, emergency maintenance activity, not deferred to a routine patch window.

2. **Approve Temporary Mitigations:** Where immediate patching is not possible (e.g., due to change control or operational constraints), authorise the security team to implement any interim mitigations recommended by Palo Alto Networks — such as network-level access restrictions to management or dataplane interfaces.

3. **Communicate Urgency Internally:** Ensure relevant IT operations, network engineering, and service desk teams are aware this is a priority item. Escalation paths should be clear if systems are found to be actively exploited.

4. **Commission a Scope Assessment:** Request confirmation from IT within **24 hours** of which firewall instances in your environment are running affected PAN-OS versions and are therefore in scope for remediation.

5. **Monitor for Exploitation:** Ask the security operations team to increase monitoring on affected firewall devices for anomalous traffic patterns or unexpected device restarts that could indicate exploitation attempts, until patching is complete.
