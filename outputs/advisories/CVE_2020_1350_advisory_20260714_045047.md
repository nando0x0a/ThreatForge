# Security Advisory: Critical Windows DNS Server Vulnerability (SIGRed)

**Advisory Reference:** CVE-2020-1350
**Issued:** For Management Review — Draft Advisory Pending Analyst Approval
**Priority Level:** 🔴 CRITICAL — ACT NOW

---

## Executive Summary

A critical, actively exploited vulnerability has been identified in Microsoft's Windows DNS Server software — a core component of nearly every Windows-based corporate network. This flaw, publicly known as "SIGRed," allows an attacker to completely take over an affected server without requiring any login credentials or physical access. It has received the highest possible severity rating and is confirmed to be actively used by malicious actors in real-world attacks. **Immediate action is required to apply Microsoft's available security update.** Delay in addressing this vulnerability places the organization at significant and direct risk of a full network compromise.

---

## Business Impact

If this vulnerability is left unaddressed, the consequences for the organization could be severe and wide-ranging:

- **Complete Network Takeover:** An attacker who successfully exploits this flaw gains the highest level of control over the affected server, with the ability to move laterally across the entire corporate network. This is equivalent to an attacker having unrestricted administrator access to your infrastructure.

- **Data Breach:** With full system-level access, attackers can access, copy, or exfiltrate sensitive business data, customer records, intellectual property, and credentials — triggering breach notification obligations under GDPR, HIPAA, and other applicable regulations.

- **Operational Disruption:** DNS servers are the "phone book" of your network. Compromise or disruption of these systems can take down email, internal applications, remote access, and internet connectivity for the entire organization.

- **Ransomware Deployment:** This type of unrestricted server access is a known entry point for ransomware gangs. A successful attack of this nature could result in the encryption of critical business systems and significant recovery costs.

- **Regulatory and Legal Exposure:** Failure to apply a known, critical, and actively exploited patch for which a fix has been available may be viewed as negligence by regulators and insurers, potentially affecting cyber insurance claims and compliance standing.

---

## Affected Systems

The following Microsoft server products are affected. Please work with your IT team to confirm whether any of these are present in your environment:

- **Windows Server 2008** (including R2 SP1)
- **Windows Server 2012** (including R2)
- **Windows Server 2016**
- **Windows Server 2019**

> ⚠️ Any Windows Server operating in a **DNS Server role** within your organization is potentially at risk. DNS servers are commonly found in corporate offices, data centres, and cloud-hosted environments. External-facing DNS servers are at the highest risk of exploitation.

---

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate the following actions immediately:**

1. **Authorize Emergency Patching:** Approve the IT and security teams to apply Microsoft's official security update to all Windows DNS servers as an emergency change, outside of normal patching cycles if necessary. The patch is available and has been for some time.

2. **Communicate Business Priority:** Direct relevant IT managers to treat this as the organization's top infrastructure priority until all affected systems are confirmed as patched and verified.

3. **Approve Temporary Mitigation if Patching is Delayed:** If any systems cannot be patched immediately due to operational constraints, approve the application of a documented temporary workaround (a registry-based configuration change) as a stop-gap measure while full patching is arranged.

4. **Request Confirmation of Completion:** Ask for a written confirmation report from the IT or security team once all affected systems have been patched, including a list of systems remediated and the date of completion.

5. **Engage Cyber Insurance:** Notify your cyber insurance provider of the vulnerability and remediation actions taken, as this may be a policy requirement in the event of a future incident.
