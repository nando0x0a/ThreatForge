# Security Advisory: Progress MOVEit Transfer — Memory Vulnerability
**Advisory Date:** Draft for Analyst Review | **Priority:** HIGH PRIORITY
**Reference:** CVE-2026-10699

---

## Executive Summary

A security vulnerability has been discovered in Progress MOVEit Transfer, a widely used file transfer platform, that allows an attacker with no login credentials or special access to disrupt the service over the internet. The vulnerability is rated **High severity** with a score of 7.5 out of 10 [1]. Specific versions released in 2025 and 2026 are affected, and a patch is already available from the vendor [2]. Immediate action is required to update affected systems before this vulnerability is exploited against your organization.

> *This advisory is a proposed draft for analyst review prior to distribution.*

---

## Business Impact

If left unaddressed, this vulnerability poses the following business risks:

- **Service Disruption:** An attacker can deliberately crash or make MOVEit Transfer unavailable, halting all managed file transfers — including those supporting partners, customers, and regulatory reporting workflows — with no need to bypass login controls.
- **Operational Risk:** Because the attack requires no credentials and can be launched from anywhere on the internet [1], the barrier for a threat actor is extremely low. The system could be targeted repeatedly, causing sustained outages.
- **Regulatory and Compliance Exposure:** MOVEit Transfer is frequently used to move sensitive and regulated data. Extended downtime of this platform may trigger breach notification obligations, SLA penalties, or audit findings depending on applicable regulatory frameworks (e.g., HIPAA, PCI-DSS, GDPR).
- **Reputational Risk:** Disruption to externally facing file transfer services could impact partner and customer confidence, particularly if MOVEit Transfer was previously associated with high-profile incidents at your organization or industry peers.

> **Note:** While the vulnerability's primary documented impact is denial of service, the vendor and NVD also indicate remote code execution (RCE) potential [1][2]. RCE would escalate risk significantly beyond disruption to potential data compromise. Analyst review is recommended to confirm RCE exploitability in your specific environment.

---

## Affected Systems

The following versions of **Progress MOVEit Transfer** are confirmed as vulnerable [2]:

| Product | Vulnerable Versions | Safe Version |
|---|---|---|
| MOVEit Transfer 2025.0.x | 2025.0.0 up to (but not including) 2025.0.8 | 2025.0.8 or later |
| MOVEit Transfer 2025.1.x | 2025.1.0 up to (but not including) 2025.1.4 | 2025.1.4 or later |
| MOVEit Transfer 2026.0.x | 2026.0.0 up to (but not including) 2026.0.1 | 2026.0.1 or later |

Any instance running a version older than the "Safe Version" listed above — whether hosted on-premises, in a private data center, or in a cloud environment — should be considered at risk.

---

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate** the following actions:

1. **Authorize Emergency Patching (Within 72 Hours):** Direct IT and infrastructure teams to identify all instances of MOVEit Transfer in the environment and apply the vendor-supplied patches immediately [2]. This is not routine maintenance — treat this as an emergency change.

2. **Confirm Scope:** Request confirmation from IT that all instances — including those managed by third parties or hosted in subsidiary environments — have been inventoried and are being addressed.

3. **Communicate to Relevant Stakeholders:** If MOVEit Transfer is used to exchange data with external partners or customers, consider proactively notifying relationship owners of a brief maintenance window to apply patches.

4. **Verify Completion:** Request written confirmation from IT leadership once all systems have been updated to a patched version, with evidence of version verification.

5. **Temporary Mitigation (If Immediate Patching Is Not Possible):** Ask IT whether access to the Custom Reports module can be restricted or disabled as a short-term measure while patching is scheduled. *Analyst confirmation of mitigation effectiveness is required before relying on this approach.*
