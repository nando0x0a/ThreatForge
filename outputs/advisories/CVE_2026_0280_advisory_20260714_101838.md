# Security Advisory: PAN-OS Firewall Policy Bypass Vulnerability
**CVE-2026-0280 | Priority Tier: HIGH PRIORITY**
*DRAFT — Proposed for analyst review before distribution*

---

## Executive Summary

A newly discovered security vulnerability in Palo Alto Networks firewall software (PAN-OS) allows unauthorized external attackers to bypass the firewall's traffic filtering rules entirely, without requiring any credentials or prior access [1][2]. By sending specially crafted network traffic, an attacker could reach internal systems and services that the firewall is designed to protect. This vulnerability was disclosed just three days ago and affects a widely deployed enterprise security product. **Immediate action is required** to assess exposure and apply available mitigations or patches.

> ⚠️ **Severity Note for Leadership:** There is a significant disagreement between two authoritative sources on how severe this vulnerability is. The U.S. National Vulnerability Database rates this as **HIGH severity (CVSS 7.2)** [1], while the CVE program's own published record (from the vendor's coordinated disclosure) rates it as **LOW severity (CVSS v4.0: 1.7)** [3]. Your security team should weigh this discrepancy carefully — the higher rating reflects network exploitability with no authentication required, while the lower rating may reflect limited real-world exploit activity reported to date [2]. Until this is resolved, we are treating this as HIGH PRIORITY out of caution.

---

## Business Impact

If this vulnerability is left unaddressed, the following business risks apply:

- **Unauthorized access to protected systems:** An attacker on the internet could bypass firewall protections and directly reach internal servers, databases, or applications that should be inaccessible from outside the organization.
- **Data breach exposure:** Protected data — including customer records, financial information, or intellectual property — could be accessed if an attacker successfully reaches internal systems through this bypass.
- **Regulatory and compliance risk:** Organizations subject to frameworks such as PCI-DSS, HIPAA, or GDPR may face compliance violations if a perimeter control as fundamental as a firewall is demonstrably circumvented, particularly if a breach results.
- **Erosion of security architecture:** Firewalls are a foundational layer of network defense. A bypass undermines the integrity of the broader security posture and may invalidate assumptions made in risk assessments or insurance evaluations.
- **Reputational risk:** A breach resulting from an unpatched, publicly known vulnerability — especially one only days old — would be difficult to defend to customers, regulators, or the board.

---

## Affected Systems

The following Palo Alto Networks products are affected [1][2]:

- **Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS** — the operating system powering Palo Alto Networks next-generation firewalls
  - Specific affected versions have not been confirmed in available sources at the time of this advisory; your security team should cross-reference the vendor's official advisory for the definitive version list [2]
- Deployments where **IPv6 network traffic is enabled or permitted** are at heightened risk, as the vulnerability is triggered through specially crafted IPv6 packets [1]

*Note: If your organization does not use IPv6 on networks protected by PAN-OS devices, your exposure may be reduced. Your security team can confirm this.*

---

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate the following actions** within the timelines below:

1. **Authorize emergency change control** to allow the security and network teams to apply vendor-released patches or workarounds to PAN-OS devices without standard change delay.
2. **Direct the security team to inventory** all PAN-OS firewall deployments and confirm which are running affected versions and have IPv6 enabled [2].
3. **Approve vendor engagement** — if a patch is not yet available or fully tested, authorize the security team to contact Palo Alto Networks support directly to obtain interim guidance [2].
4. **Communicate urgency to IT and network operations leadership** so that patching is treated as a priority work item, not deferred to a regular maintenance window.
5. **Request a status update** from the security team within 48 hours confirming that all internet-facing PAN-OS devices have been assessed and either patched or mitigated.
