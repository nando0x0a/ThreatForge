# Security Advisory: Stored Cross-Site Scripting in Progress MOVEit Transfer
**Advisory Date:** (Draft for Analyst Review)
**CVE Reference:** CVE-2026-11903
**Priority Tier:** STANDARD | **Severity:** HIGH (CVSS 8.0) [1]

---

## Executive Summary

A security vulnerability has been identified in Progress MOVEit Transfer, a widely used managed file transfer platform. The vulnerability allows a malicious actor to embed harmful scripts into the application's Ad Hoc file-sharing module, which are then executed when another user — such as an employee or trusted partner — interacts with the affected content. The flaw is rated **High severity** [1] and affects several recent versions of the software [2]. A fix is available from the vendor and should be applied in an orderly, planned manner. No evidence of active exploitation has been identified at this time, and the attack requires a user to take an action (such as clicking a link or opening a page), which moderately limits the immediate risk.

---

## Business Impact

If left unaddressed, this vulnerability could expose the organisation to the following risks:

- **Data Theft:** A successful attack could allow a malicious actor to steal session credentials or sensitive information from employees or partners who use the MOVEit Transfer portal, potentially enabling unauthorised access to files being transferred through the platform.
- **Account Compromise:** Hijacked user sessions could grant an attacker the same access privileges as the targeted employee, including access to confidential business documents, contracts, or regulated data in transit.
- **Regulatory and Compliance Exposure:** MOVEit Transfer is frequently used to handle sensitive or regulated data (e.g., personal data, financial records, health information). A breach originating from this vulnerability could trigger notification obligations under GDPR, HIPAA, or other applicable frameworks, with associated reputational and financial consequences.
- **Supply Chain / Partner Trust Risk:** The Ad Hoc module is used to share files with external parties. A compromised interaction could affect not just internal staff but also clients and partners, damaging trust and business relationships.

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate the following actions** to the relevant IT and security teams:

1. **Authorise Scheduled Patching:** Approve the application of vendor-released security updates to MOVEit Transfer within the timeline outlined below. The patch is available from Progress [2] and should be applied during a scheduled maintenance window to minimise disruption.
2. **Request Confirmation of Version Status:** Ask your IT team to confirm, within **48 hours**, whether your organisation is running an affected version and to provide a patching schedule.
3. **Communicate to Key Stakeholders:** If MOVEit Transfer is used to share files with external clients or partners via the Ad Hoc module, consider informing those parties that a security update is being applied as a precautionary measure.
4. **Review Access Logs (Optional but Recommended):** Ask the security team to perform a brief review of recent MOVEit Transfer activity logs to check for any anomalous behaviour, particularly in the Ad Hoc module, as a precautionary step.

> ⚠️ *Note: The vendor advisory retrieved during preparation of this document contained limited technical detail due to an access-restricted customer portal [2]. The IT security team should consult the full Progress Customer Community advisory directly to confirm patch applicability and any additional mitigations.*
