# Security Advisory: Palo Alto Networks Firewall Policy Bypass Vulnerability
**CVE-2026-0280 | Priority Tier: HIGH PRIORITY | Issued: [Date] — DRAFT FOR ANALYST REVIEW**

---

## Executive Summary

A newly discovered vulnerability in Palo Alto Networks firewall software (PAN-OS) allows an unauthenticated attacker on the internet to bypass the organization's firewall rules entirely by sending specially crafted network traffic [2]. This means that systems and services we rely on our firewall to protect could potentially be reached by attackers without authorization, as though the firewall were not there. This vulnerability was disclosed only three days ago [1] and affects a widely deployed, business-critical security control. **Immediate action is required** to assess exposure and apply available mitigations.

> **⚠️ Severity Discrepancy — Analyst Attention Required:** Two authoritative sources rate this vulnerability differently. The U.S. National Vulnerability Database (NVD) scores it 7.2 out of 10 (HIGH) [1], while the vulnerability record published directly by the vendor/CNA rates it 1.7 out of 10 (LOW) using a newer scoring standard [3]. This disagreement has not been resolved and may reflect differences in scoring methodology or assumed deployment context. Until clarified, the security team recommends treating this as HIGH priority given the nature of the bypass and network-accessible attack method.

---

## Business Impact

If this vulnerability is left unaddressed, the following business risks apply:

- **Unauthorized Access to Protected Systems:** Attackers could reach internal servers, databases, or applications that are specifically intended to be shielded behind our firewall, bypassing a core layer of our defense [2].
- **Data Breach Exposure:** Services holding sensitive customer, financial, or operational data that depend on firewall protection could be directly targeted, increasing the risk of a data breach and associated regulatory penalties (e.g., under GDPR, HIPAA, or PCI-DSS).
- **Service Disruption:** Once protected services are reachable, attackers could attempt to exploit them further, potentially causing outages or ransomware incidents affecting business operations.
- **Erosion of Security Controls:** A firewall bypass undermines the effectiveness of all security investments downstream of this control, potentially invalidating compliance postures that assume network segmentation is enforced.
- **No Warning Signs:** Because this is a bypass technique rather than a brute-force attack, it may not trigger standard security alerts, making unauthorized access harder to detect.

## Affected Systems

Based on available advisory information [2], the following Palo Alto Networks products are affected:

- **Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS** — the operating system running on Palo Alto Networks firewall appliances and virtual firewalls
  - Specific affected version ranges have not been fully detailed in available sources at this time; the security team is actively confirming which PAN-OS versions deployed in our environment fall within scope

> **Note to Analyst:** Vendor advisory should be reviewed directly at [2] to confirm exact affected version numbers before distributing this advisory further.

## Recommended Action

Management is asked to **approve and communicate** the following actions immediately:

1. **Authorize Emergency Change Window (Within 24–48 Hours):** Allow the security and infrastructure teams to apply vendor-supplied patches or recommended workarounds to all affected PAN-OS firewalls during an expedited maintenance window, outside of normal change freeze periods if necessary.

2. **Approve Targeted Traffic Restriction:** Authorize the network team to implement interim controls — such as restricting or monitoring IPv6 traffic flows at affected perimeter points — as a temporary protective measure while patching is completed [2].

3. **Direct Inventory Confirmation (Within 24 Hours):** Instruct IT asset owners to provide an immediate confirmed list of all PAN-OS firewalls in production, including version numbers, so the security team can quantify full exposure.

4. **Escalate Monitoring:** Approve elevated monitoring posture on traffic traversing affected firewalls for the next 30 days, with instructions to escalate any anomalous access to protected segments.

5. **Communicate to Risk and Compliance Teams:** Given the potential compliance implications of a firewall bypass, notify the Risk and Legal/Compliance functions so they can assess whether any notification obligations are triggered.
