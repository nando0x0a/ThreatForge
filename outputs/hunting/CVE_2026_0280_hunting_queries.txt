## CrowdStrike Event Search

### Query 1 — Anomalous IPv6 Network Connections from/to PAN-OS-Adjacent Hosts

This targets hosts that may sit behind or adjacent to a PAN-OS firewall and are receiving connections that should have been blocked by policy — indicative of a bypass having occurred.

event_simpleName=NetworkConnect
| eval IsIPv6=if(match(RemoteIP, "^[0-9a-fA-F:]+:[0-9a-fA-F:]+$"), "true", "false")
| where IsIPv6="true"
| where RemotePort IN (22, 23, 80, 443, 3389, 445, 8080, 8443)
| stats count as ConnectionCount, values(RemotePort) as Ports,
        earliest(timestamp) as FirstSeen, latest(timestamp) as LastSeen
        by ComputerName, UserName, RemoteIP, LocalIP
| where ConnectionCount < 5
| eval Anomaly="Low-frequency IPv6 connection to sensitive port — possible policy bypass artefact"
| sort -ConnectionCount

> **Analyst note:** Low-count IPv6 connections to high-value ports from unexpected source addresses may indicate traffic that bypassed PAN-OS security policy via the CVE-2026-0280 dataplane vulnerability [2]. Tune `RemotePort` list to match your protected service inventory.

### Query 2 — Post-Exploitation Process Execution on Hosts Behind the Firewall

Targets command execution patterns consistent with an attacker who has reached a protected service after bypassing the firewall. Focus on processes spawned from network-facing services.

event_simpleName IN ("ProcessRollup2", "SyntheticProcessRollup2")
| where ParentBaseFileName IN (
    "nginx", "apache2", "httpd", "tomcat", "java",
    "python", "python3", "ruby", "node", "perl"
  )
| where FileName IN (
    "sh", "bash", "dash", "zsh", "cmd.exe", "powershell.exe",
    "whoami", "id", "uname", "ifconfig", "ip", "netstat",
    "curl", "wget", "nc", "ncat", "socat"
  )
| stats count as ExecCount,
        values(CommandLine) as Commands,
        earliest(timestamp) as FirstSeen,
        latest(timestamp) as LastSeen
        by ComputerName, UserName, ParentBaseFileName, FileName
| where ExecCount < 10
| eval EscalationFlag=if(ExecCount=1, "SINGLE_EXECUTION — HIGH SUSPICION", "REVIEW")
| sort -EscalationFlag, -ExecCount

> **Analyst note:** Shell or recon tool execution spawned from web-facing parent processes on hosts that should be protected by the PAN-OS policy boundary is a post-bypass exploitation indicator. Single-execution events (ExecCount=1) are highest priority. No confirmed post-exploitation TTPs are published for CVE-2026-0280 at this time — these patterns reflect general network-exploitation post-compromise behaviour.

### Query 3 — IPv6 Source Address Anomalies in DNS Resolution (Potential C2 Pivot)

After bypassing the firewall to reach a host, an attacker may establish outbound connectivity. This query looks for DNS queries to non-internal resolvers over IPv6, which may indicate post-compromise C2 beaconing.

event_simpleName=DnsRequest
| eval IsIPv6Resolver=if(match(ContextBaseFileName, ".*") AND
    match(DomainName, "^(?!.*\\.internal$)(?!.*\\.corp$)(?!.*\\.local$).*\\.[a-z]{2,}$"), "true", "false")
| where IsIPv6Resolver="true"
| where NOT DomainName IN (
    "*.microsoft.com", "*.windowsupdate.com", "*.google.com",
    "*.amazonaws.com", "*.cloudfront.net"
  )
| stats count as QueryCount,
        values(DomainName) as QueriedDomains,
        dc(DomainName) as UniqueDomains,
        earliest(timestamp) as FirstSeen,
        latest(timestamp) as LastSeen
        by ComputerName, UserName
| where QueryCount > 20 AND UniqueDomains < 3
| eval BeaconingFlag="High-repetition low-diversity DNS — possible C2 beaconing pattern"
| sort -QueryCount

> **Analyst note:** High query volume to a small set of non-corporate domains is a beaconing heuristic. Substitute `*.internal`, `*.corp`, `*.local` with your organisation's actual internal domain suffixes. Exclude known-good CDNs and cloud providers relevant to your environment.

## nfdump Netflow Queries

### Query 1 — IPv6 Traffic to Protected Services on Exploit-Relevant Ports

Targets inbound IPv6 flows reaching hosts that should be blocked by PAN-OS policy. Presence of such flows may indicate the bypass is actively being used.

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'inet6 and proto tcp \
      and dst port in [22 23 80 443 445 3389 8080 8443] \
      and src net not [fd00::/8] \
      and src net not [fc00::/7]' \
  -o "fmt:%ts %te %sa %da %sp %dp %pkt %byt %fl" \
  -s record/packets \
  -n 50 \
  -O bytes

> **Analyst note:** `fd00::/8` and `fc00::/7` exclude RFC 4193 ULA (private) IPv6 space. Adjust exclusions for your network's assigned IPv6 prefixes. Flows appearing on ports that your PAN-OS policy should block are the primary indicator of a bypass [2].
