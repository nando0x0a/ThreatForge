## CrowdStrike Event Search

### Query 1 — Outbound Network Connections from PAN-OS Management Hosts to Suspicious Destinations
*Targets C2 or exfiltration connections initiated from hosts associated with PAN-OS management infrastructure following potential exploitation.*

event_simpleName=NetworkConnectIP4
| search (RemotePort IN ("443", "80", "4444", "8080", "1337", "9001"))
| search ComputerName IN ("<panos-mgmt-hostname-1>", "<panos-mgmt-hostname-2>")
| where NOT (RemoteIP IN ("<known-legitimate-update-server-ip>", "<siem-ip>", "<management-network-range>"))
| eval ConnectionTime=formatTime("%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%S", ContextTimeStamp_decimal)
| stats count AS ConnectionCount, values(RemotePort) AS Ports, values(RemoteIP) AS RemoteIPs
    by ComputerName, UserName, ConnectionTime
| where ConnectionCount < 5
| sort -ConnectionTime
> 📝 **Analyst note:** Replace `<panos-mgmt-hostname-*>` with your PAN-OS management plane hostnames. Populate the `NOT IN` allowlist with known Palo Alto update and telemetry IPs. Low-count connections are more suspicious than high-volume bulk traffic in this context.

### Query 2 — Suspicious Child Process Spawning from PAN-OS Management Plane Processes
*Command injection via the CLI [1][2] may result in unexpected OS-level child processes being spawned under PAN-OS management parent processes. This query hunts for shells or system utilities launched from atypical parents.*

event_simpleName=ProcessRollup2
| search ParentBaseFileName IN ("mgmtsrvr", "pan_task", "configd", "routed", "sslvpn", "pan_python")
| search FileName IN ("sh", "bash", "dash", "ksh", "python", "python3", "perl", "wget", "curl",
                      "nc", "ncat", "nmap", "id", "whoami", "uname", "iptables", "crontab",
                      "chmod", "chown", "useradd", "passwd", "scp", "ssh", "base64")
| eval SpawnTime=formatTime("%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%S", ContextTimeStamp_decimal)
| stats count AS SpawnCount, values(CommandLine) AS CommandLines, values(FileName) AS ChildProcesses
    by ComputerName, UserName, ParentBaseFileName, SpawnTime
| sort -SpawnCount
> 📝 **Analyst note:** PAN-OS management process names listed are indicative — validate against your PAN-OS version's actual process list. Any shell or recon utility spawned under a PAN-OS management parent warrants immediate escalation given the command injection nature of this CVE [1][2]. `CommandLine` values should be reviewed for injection syntax (semicolons, pipes, backticks, `$(...)` subshell patterns).

### Query 3 — Administrator Account Activity Anomalies on PAN-OS Management Plane
*Since exploitation requires administrator authentication [1], hunting for anomalous admin session behaviour (off-hours access, new source IPs, rapid command sequences) may surface pre- or post-exploitation activity.*

event_simpleName IN ("UserLogon", "UserLogonFailed", "NetworkConnectIP4")
| search ComputerName IN ("<panos-mgmt-hostname-1>", "<panos-mgmt-hostname-2>")
| eval LogonHour=tonumber(strftime("%H", ContextTimeStamp_decimal))
| eval IsOffHours=if(LogonHour < 7 OR LogonHour > 19, "YES", "NO")
| eval EventTime=formatTime("%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%S", ContextTimeStamp_decimal)
| stats count AS EventCount, values(IsOffHours) AS OffHoursActivity,
         values(RemoteIP) AS SourceIPs, dc(RemoteIP) AS UniqueSourceIPs
    by ComputerName, UserName, event_simpleName, EventTime
| where IsOffHours="YES" OR UniqueSourceIPs > 3
| sort -EventTime
> 📝 **Analyst note:** Adjust off-hours thresholds to your organisation's operational window. Multiple unique source IPs for a single admin account in a short window may indicate credential compromise or session hijacking used as a precursor to CLI injection.

## nfdump Netflow Queries

### Query 1 — Outbound Traffic from PAN-OS Management IPs to Suspicious Destinations on Common C2 Ports
*Post-exploitation command injection as root [1] may result in reverse shells or C2 beacons egressing from management plane IPs.*

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'proto tcp \
      and src ip <panos-mgmt-ip> \
      and dst port in [443,80,4444,8080,1337,9001,53] \
      and not dst ip in [<legitimate-update-server-ip>/32,<siem-ip>/32,<monitoring-subnet>/24]' \
  -o 'fmt:%ts %sa %da %sp %dp %pkt %byt %fl %pr' \
  -s record/bytes \
  -n 50
> 📝 **Analyst note:** Replace `<panos-mgmt-ip>` with your management interface IP(s). Port 53 is included as DNS-over-UDP tunnelling is a common post-exploitation egress technique. Flows to previously unseen external IPs from management infrastructure are high-priority findings.
