### Query 1 — Network Connections to Dataplane Interface IPs / Known Malicious Sources

Targets outbound or lateral connections from PAN-OS management hosts or adjacent systems toward known malicious IPs associated with this campaign. Replace placeholder IPs with confirmed threat intelligence.

event_simpleName=NetworkConnect
| search RemoteIP IN ("x.x.x.x", "y.y.y.y")
    OR (RemotePort IN ("80", "443", "22", "4444", "8443")
        AND LocalAddressIP4 IN ("PAN_MGMT_IP_1", "PAN_MGMT_IP_2"))
| eval ConnectionSummary=strcat(ComputerName, " -> ", RemoteIP, ":", RemotePort)
| stats
    count AS ConnectionCount,
    earliest(_time) AS FirstSeen,
    latest(_time) AS LastSeen
    BY ComputerName, UserName, RemoteIP, RemotePort, ConnectionSummary
| sort -ConnectionCount
| where ConnectionCount > 3

### Query 2 — Post-Exploitation Process Execution Patterns on Adjacent Hosts

If a DoS causes failover or misconfiguration, adjacent systems may be targeted. This query hunts for suspicious process chains that could indicate post-exploitation following a successful disruption of network controls.

event_simpleName IN ("ProcessRollup2", "SyntheticProcessRollup2")
| search
    (ParentBaseFileName IN ("python.exe", "python3", "sh", "bash", "cmd.exe", "powershell.exe")
     AND FileName IN ("nc", "ncat", "curl", "wget", "whoami", "id", "ifconfig", "ip"))
    OR (FileName IN ("meterpreter", "beacon.exe", "agent.exe"))
| eval ProcessChain=strcat(ParentBaseFileName, " -> ", FileName)
| stats
    count AS ExecutionCount,
    values(CommandLine) AS CommandLines,
    earliest(_time) AS FirstSeen,
    latest(_time) AS LastSeen
    BY ComputerName, UserName, ProcessChain, FileName
| sort -ExecutionCount
| where ExecutionCount < 10

> **Note:** Because the described vulnerability is a DoS affecting PAN-OS dataplane processing [1][2], direct RCE on the firewall itself is not confirmed in vendor advisory language — the RCE flag in the priority metadata should be treated cautiously pending clarification. Post-exploitation queries are included as a precautionary tier given the unauthenticated network vector [1].

### Query 3 — Repeated High-Volume Inbound Connection Spikes to Firewall Interfaces

Detects hosts generating anomalously high outbound connection counts toward PAN-OS dataplane addresses, consistent with crafted traffic floods described in the advisory [2].

event_simpleName=NetworkConnect
| search RemoteIP IN ("PAN_DATAPLANE_IP_1", "PAN_DATAPLANE_IP_2")
| bucket _time span=1m
| stats
    count AS ConnectionsPerMinute,
    dc(RemotePort) AS UniquePortsTargeted,
    values(RemotePort) AS PortList
    BY _time, ComputerName, RemoteIP
| where ConnectionsPerMinute > 200 OR UniquePortsTargeted > 20
| sort -ConnectionsPerMinute

## nfdump Netflow Queries

### Query 1 — Traffic to PAN-OS Dataplane IPs on Exploit-Relevant Ports

Targets flow records destined for PAN-OS dataplane interface IPs. Replace `x.x.x.x` with actual dataplane interface addresses. Port scope should be refined based on which PAN-OS services are exposed on your dataplane [2].

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'dst ip x.x.x.x and (proto tcp or proto udp) and \
      (dst port 80 or dst port 443 or dst port 22 or dst port 8443 or dst port 4444)' \
  -o "fmt:%ts %sa %sp %da %dp %pr %byt %pkt %fl" \
  -s record/packets \
  -n 50

> **Note:** The advisory describes exploitation via **specially crafted network traffic** to dataplane interfaces [2]. Prioritise flows with unusually high packet rates and low byte-per-packet ratios, which may indicate malformed or fragmented packet floods.

### Query 2 — Anomalous Traffic Volume Patterns — Potential DoS Flood Detection

Identifies source IPs sending abnormally high packet volumes to PAN-OS dataplane addresses within a short aggregation window, consistent with a volumetric or crafted-packet DoS [1][2].

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'dst ip x.x.x.x and proto tcp' \
  -A srcip,dstport \
  -o "fmt:%sa %dp %byt %pkt" \
  -s record/packets \
  -n 30 | awk '$4 > 10000 {print $0}'

Supplement with a time-sliced aggregation to spot burst patterns:

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'dst ip x.x.x.x' \
  -t 2026/01/01.00:00:00-2026/12/31.23:59:59 \
  -A srcip \
  -s record/bytes \
  -o long \
  -n 25

> Replace the `-t` time range with your relevant investigation window.
