## CrowdStrike Event Search

### Query 1 — Network Connections from PAN-OS Management Hosts to Anomalous External IPs
Targets unexpected outbound connections from systems in the PAN-OS management network segment — may indicate post-exploitation callback if a device is compromised beyond DoS.

event_simpleName=NetworkConnect
| eval RemoteIP_str=if(isnotnull(RemoteIP), RemoteIP, "unknown")
| search RemoteIP IN ("x.x.x.x")
  OR (RemotePort IN ("4444","8080","1337","9001","443"))
| where NOT (RemoteIP LIKE "10.%"
          OR RemoteIP LIKE "192.168.%"
          OR RemoteIP LIKE "172.16.%")
| stats
    count AS connection_count,
    values(RemotePort) AS ports_contacted,
    values(RemoteIP) AS remote_ips,
    earliest(_time) AS first_seen,
    latest(_time) AS last_seen
  by ComputerName, UserName, LocalIP
| where connection_count > 5
| sort -connection_count

> **Analyst note:** Replace `x.x.x.x` with confirmed attacker IPs when available. The port list targets common reverse-shell and C2 ports; adjust to your environment's baseline.

### Query 2 — Process Execution Anomalies on PAN-OS Adjacent Linux Hosts (Post-Exploitation Indicator)
Targets shell spawning, credential access, and lateral movement tooling that may follow a successful exploitation of a firewall management interface. Applicable to Linux-based management jump hosts or orchestration systems near PAN-OS devices.

event_simpleName IN ("ProcessRollup2","SyntheticProcessRollup2")
| search (
    FileName IN ("bash","sh","python","python3","curl","wget","nc","ncat","socat","chmod","base64")
    AND ParentBaseFileName IN ("sshd","httpd","nginx","openssl","python","python3")
  )
| eval suspicious_combo=if(
    (FileName IN ("bash","sh") AND ParentBaseFileName IN ("python","python3","openssl")),
    "HIGH_SUSPICION", "REVIEW"
  )
| stats
    count AS exec_count,
    values(CommandLine) AS command_lines,
    values(FileName) AS child_processes,
    earliest(_time) AS first_seen,
    latest(_time) AS last_seen
  by ComputerName, UserName, ParentBaseFileName, suspicious_combo
| sort -exec_count

> **Analyst note:** PAN-OS itself runs on a proprietary OS and may not surface directly in CrowdStrike EDR telemetry. This query targets adjacent Linux management hosts. Tune `ParentBaseFileName` to match your actual management-plane process inventory.

### Query 3 — High-Volume Outbound Traffic Bursts (Dataplane DoS Artefact — Host-Side Visibility)
Looks for hosts generating or receiving unusually high connection rates, which may correlate with dataplane flooding behaviour described in the vulnerability [1][2].

event_simpleName=NetworkConnect
| bin _time span=1m
| stats
    count AS connections_per_minute,
    dc(RemoteIP) AS unique_remote_ips,
    values(RemotePort) AS ports,
    values(Protocol) AS protocols
  by _time, ComputerName, LocalIP
| where connections_per_minute > 500 OR unique_remote_ips > 100
| eval anomaly_reason=case(
    connections_per_minute > 1000, "EXTREME_CONNECTION_RATE",
    unique_remote_ips > 200,       "WIDE_FAN_OUT",
    true(),                        "ELEVATED_RATE"
  )
| sort -connections_per_minute

> **Analyst note:** Thresholds (500 cpm, 100 unique IPs) are indicative starting points. Baseline your environment's normal PAN-OS management traffic before applying. High rates may also reflect legitimate load balancer or scanner traffic.

## nfdump Netflow Queries

### Query 1 — Traffic Directed at PAN-OS Dataplane Interface IPs on Known Service Ports
Targets traffic matching the exploit's delivery mechanism — specially crafted packets to dataplane interfaces [1][2].

nfdump -r /var/log/netflow/nfcapd.current \
  -f 'dst ip x.x.x.x and \
      (proto tcp or proto udp) and \
      (dst port 443 or dst port 80 or dst port 8443 or dst port 8080 or \
       dst port 22 or dst port 3978)' \
  -s record/bytes \
  -n 50 \
  -o "fmt:%ts %td %pr %sa:%sp -> %da:%dp %pkt %byt %fl"

> **Analyst note:** Replace `x.x.x.x` with the actual IP addresses of your PAN-OS dataplane interfaces. Port list reflects common PAN-OS service ports; adjust to your deployment. The vulnerability targets dataplane interfaces specifically [2], so management-plane IPs warrant separate monitoring.
