### Severity Note
This vulnerability carries a CVSS score of **9.6 (CRITICAL)** [1]. The stored XSS variant is particularly concerning because:
- Exploitation requires **no special privileges** [1][3], lowering attacker barrier.
- Stored XSS in Exchange OWA can enable **session hijacking, credential theft, and spoofing** against victims who merely view a malicious email or calendar item.
- The network-accessible nature means internet-facing Exchange deployments are at heightened risk.

### Recommended Detection Approach (Pending Confirmed IoCs)
Until specific IoCs are available, analysts should focus on:
1. **Web Application Firewall (WAF) logs** — look for anomalous script-like content (`<script>`, `javascript:`, `onerror=`, `onload=`) in POST bodies to OWA/ECP endpoints.
2. **Exchange IIS logs** — unusual responses or large POST payloads to `/owa/` or `/ecp/` from unauthenticated or low-privilege sources.
3. **Browser-side CSP violation reports** — if Content Security Policy is deployed on Exchange, XSS trigger attempts may generate violations.
4. **Patch status verification** — prioritize patching per Microsoft guidance [2] given the CRITICAL rating and no-privilege-required attack vector [1].
