## Observable Behaviour Patterns

Given the nature of CVE-2026-6896, defenders should monitor for the following behavioural patterns in lieu of confirmed IoCs:

**Confidence: MEDIUM**

1. **Unusual script execution in browser sessions** — Users reporting unexpected pop-ups, redirections, or session anomalies after viewing GitLab issues, MRs, wikis, or other developer-created content on affected versions (GitLab EE ≥ 13.11, < 18.11.7; 19.0.x < 19.0.4; 19.1.x < 19.1.2) [1][4].

2. **Suspicious content injected by developer-role accounts** — Review recently created or modified issues, merge requests, wiki pages, and snippets authored by developer-role users for obfuscated JavaScript payloads (e.g., `<script>`, `javascript:`, `onerror=`, `onload=`, `data:text/html`, SVG-embedded scripts, HTML entity-encoded variants).

3. **Anomalous session token or cookie exfiltration attempts** — Inspect web application firewall (WAF) and proxy logs for outbound requests from GitLab-rendered pages to unexpected external domains, particularly carrying session cookies or tokens.

4. **CSP violation reports** — If Content Security Policy reporting is enabled on your GitLab instance, an uptick in CSP violation reports tied to developer-editable content pages may be an early indicator of injection attempts.

5. **Privilege escalation sequences** — Monitor for developer-role accounts that subsequently access resources consistent with higher-privilege roles (Owner, Maintainer), which may indicate successful session hijacking via XSS.
